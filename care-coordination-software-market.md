<p><p>Care coordination software is categorized into two main types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions. Cloud-based software operates on remote servers, enabling real-time access from any internet-connected device, which fosters collaboration among care teams and offers scalable solutions. On-premises software, conversely, is installed directly on a healthcare provider's local servers, providing control over data security and customization, but may require significant infrastructure investment and limited accessibility. Each type presents unique benefits tailored to different organizational needs and operational environments.</p></p>
